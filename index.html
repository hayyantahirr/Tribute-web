The Founder Of Pakistan

Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan, was a visionary leader and statesman who played a pivotal role in the subcontinent's history. Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, Jinnah was a skilled lawyer and a determined advocate for Indian Muslims' rights. His tireless efforts and unyielding resolve led to the creation of Pakistan in 1947. Revered as "Quaid-e-Azam" (Great Leader), Jinnah's principles of unity, faith, and discipline continue to inspire millions across the globe.

Biographies

  • Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, Jinnah was the eldest of seven siblings.
  • He studied law in London, becoming the youngest Indian to pass the bar exam at age 20.
  • A successful lawyer, he gained fame for his integrity and eloquence in legal and political matters.
  • Jinnah initially joined the Indian National Congress, advocating for Hindu-Muslim unity.
  • In 1913, he joined the Muslim League, becoming its leader and advocating for Muslim rights.
  • He played a pivotal role in the Lahore Resolution of 1940, calling for a separate state for Muslims.
  • On August 14, 1947, Jinnah achieved his goal with the creation of Pakistan, becoming its first Governor-General.
  • Known as "Quaid-e-Azam" (Great Leader), Jinnah's leadership remains a symbol of determination and unity.
